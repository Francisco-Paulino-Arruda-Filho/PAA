\( \text{Questão 01: } T(n)=4T(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor)+cn \)

\( \text{Desconsidere pisos e tetos e vamos supor que \(n=2^k\) } \)

\[ \begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \text{Nível \(i\)} & \text{Tamanho da entrada} & \text{Nós} & \text{Tempo nó} \\ \hline 1 & n & 1 & cn \\ 2 & \frac{n}{2} & 4 & \frac{cn}{2} \\ 3 & \frac{n}{4} & 16 & \frac{cn}{4} \\ 4 & \frac{n}{8} & 64 & \frac{cn}{8} \\ x & 1 & 4^i & 1 \\ \hline \end{array} \]

\( n = 2^k \)

\( \log^n_2 = k \)

\[ \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|} \hline \text{Nível \(i\)} & \text{Tamanho da entrada} & \text{Nós} & \text{Tempo nó} \\ \hline 1 & n & 1 & cn \\ 2 & \frac{n}{2} & 4 & \frac{cn}{2} \\ 3 & \frac{n}{4} & 16 & \frac{cn}{4} \\ 4 & \frac{n}{8} & 64 & \frac{cn}{8} \\ \log_2^n & 1 & 4^{\log_2^n} & 1 \\ \hline \end{array} \]

\( 4^{\log_2^n} = 2^{\log_2^n} * 2^{\log_2^n} = n * n = n^2 \)

\[ \begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \text{Nível \(i\)} & \text{Tamanho da entrada} & \text{Nós} & \text{Tempo nó} \\ \hline 1 & n & 1 & cn \\ 2 & \frac{n}{2} & 4 & \frac{cn}{2} \\ 3 & \frac{n}{4} & 16 & \frac{cn}{4} \\ 4 & \frac{n}{8} & 64 & \frac{cn}{8} \\ log_2^n & 1 & n^2 & 1 \\ \hline \end{array} \]

\( 4^i\frac{n}{2^i} \)

\( 4^i = 2^{2*i} = 2^{2i} \)

\( \text{Termo geral:} \)

\( \sum_{i=0}^{\log_2^n-1} cn4^i * \frac{n}{2^i} \)

\( cn\sum_{i=0}^{\log_2^n-1} 4^i * \frac{n}{2^i} \)

\( cn\sum_{i=0}^{\log_2^n-1} 2^{2i} * \frac{n}{2^i} \)

\( cn\sum_0^{\log_2^n-1} 2^i \)

\( \text{Soma total:} \)

\( n^2 + cn\sum_0^{\log_2^n-1} 2^i \)

\( \text{\(n^2\) é o último termo o somatório vai até \(\log_2^n-1\)} \)

\( \text{Fórmula: \(\sum_{i=0}^k n^i = \frac{n^{i+1}-1}{n-1} \) } \)

\( n^2 + cn\sum_0^{\log_2^n-1} 2^i = n^2 + cn(\frac{2^{\log_2^n-1+1}-1}{2-1}) \)

\( n^2 + cn(2^{\log_2^n}-1) \)

\( n^2 + cn(n-1) \)

\( n^2 + cn^2 - cn \)

\( \text{Logo, \(T(n) = \Theta(n^2)\)} \)

\( \text{Questão 02: Verifique o limite superior obtido na questão anterior usando o método de substituição.} \)

\( \text{Caso base: } \)

\( \text{A recorrência \(T(n)=4T(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor) + cn\) temos um caso base T(1) = x} \)

\( T(1) = x \leq d(1)^2 - e(1) = d - e \)

\( c leq d - e \)

\( T(1) \leq c*1^2 \)

\( 1 \leq c \)

\( \text{Escolha \(c_1 \geq 1\)} \)

\( \text{Caso base é verdadeiro} \)

\( \text{Queremos provar que \(T(n) = O(n^2)\)} \)

\( \text{H.I: Suponha que \(T(k) \leq ck^2\) para todo \(n_0 \leq k < n\)} \)

\( \text{Passo indutivo} \)

\( T(n)=4T(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor)+cn \)

\( T(n) \leq 4c(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor)^2 + cn \)

\( 4c(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor)^2 + cn \leq 4c(\frac{n}{2})^2 + cn \)

\( T(n) \leq 4c(\frac{n}{2})^2 + cn \)

\( T(n) \leq 4c(\frac{n^2}{4}) + cn \)

\( T(n) \leq cn^2 + cn \)

\( cn^2 + cn \leq cn^2 \)

\( \text{É necessário fortalecer a hipótese} \)

\( \text{H.I: Suponha que \(T(k) \leq ck^2 - dk\) para todo \(n_0 \leq k < n\)} \)

\( \text{Passo indutivo} \)

\( T(n) = 4T(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor) + cn \)

\( T(n) \leq 4(c(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor)^2 - d(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor))+ cn \)

\( 4((c\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor)^2 - d(\lfloor \frac{n}{2} \rfloor))+ cn \leq 4((\frac{n}{2})^2 - d(\frac{n}{2})) + cn \)

\( T(n) \leq 4(c(\frac{n}{2})^2 - d(\frac{n}{2})) + cn \)

\( T(n) \leq 4(c\frac{n^2}{4} - \frac{dn}{2}) + cn \)

\( T(n) \leq cn^2 - 2dn + cn \)

\( cn^2 - 2dn + cn \leq cn^2 - dn \)

\( 2dn - cn \leq -dn \)

\( -cn \leq -3dn \)

\( c \geq 3d \)

\( d \leq \frac{c}{3} \)

\( \text{Logo, \(T(n) = O(n^2)\)} \)
